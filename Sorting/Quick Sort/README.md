# ⚡ Quick Sort

*Choose a pivot, partition the array, and sort recursively.*

</div>

---

## 📖 Overview

Quick Sort also uses the **Divide and Conquer** approach. It selects a pivot element, places it in its correct position, and recursively sorts the remaining subarrays.

It is one of the fastest sorting algorithms in practice.

---

## ⚡ Complexity

| Best | Average | Worst | Space |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| O(n log n) | O(n log n) | O(n²) | O(log n) |

**Stable:** ❌ No  
**In-place:** ✅ Yes

---

## ✅ Pros

- Very fast in practice
- In-place sorting
- Efficient for large datasets

---

## ❌ Cons

- Worst-case time complexity is **O(n²)**
- Not a stable sorting algorithm

---

## 💡 Key Takeaway

> Quick Sort is often the preferred general-purpose sorting algorithm due to its excellent average-case performance, despite its worst-case complexity.
